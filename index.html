About Virat Kohli

A Glimpse into the Life and Career of the Modern Cricketing Icon

Early Life

Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Virat Kohli grew up in a middle-class family. His father, Prem Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer, while his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a homemaker. From a young age, Kohli showed a keen interest in cricket, and his family supported his passion, enrolling him in cricket coaching classes.

Career Beginnings

Kohli's talent was evident early on, and he quickly rose through the ranks of Delhi's cricket circuit. He made his first-class debut for Delhi in 2006 and soon caught the attention of selectors with his consistent performances. In 2008, he captained the Indian U-19 team to victory in the U-19 World Cup, showcasing his leadership skills and cricketing acumen.

International Career

Virat Kohli made his international debut for India in 2008, and since then, there has been no looking back. He has shattered numerous records and amassed runs across all formats of the game. Kohli's ability to perform under pressure and his hunger for runs have made him a cricketing legend.

Captaincy

In 2013, Kohli was appointed the vice-captain of the Indian cricket team, and he took over the captaincy across all formats in 2017 after the retirement of MS Dhoni. Under his leadership, India has achieved numerous milestones, including a historic Test series win in Australia in 2018-19.

Virat Kohli's Remarkable Achievements

Achievements

ICC ODI Player of the Year:

Kohli received this prestigious award in 2012, 2017, and 2018.


Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy (ICC Cricketer of the Year):

He was honored with this trophy in 2017 and 2018.

ICC Test Player of the Year:

In 2018, Kohli was recognized as the best Test player by the ICC.


Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World:

Kohli achieved this title in 2016, 2017, and 2018.

Highest Run-Scorer:

Kohli holds the record for the highest run-scorer in T20Is and the Indian Premier League (IPL). He ranks third in ODIs and stands fourth-highest in international cricket overall.


Double Centuries as Test Captain:

Kohli scored six double-centuries as a captain in Test matches, surpassing cricket legend Brian Lara’s record of five.

150+ Scores as Test Captain:

He has achieved scores of 150 or more as captain in Tests nine times.


Consecutive Centuries Against Two Opponents:

Kohli became the first player to hit consecutive centuries against West Indies and Sri Lanka.

Most Successful Indian ODI Captain:

Kohli’s success rate as an ODI captain is 75.89%, surpassing even M.S. Dhoni.


20,000+ Runs Across Formats:

Kohli scored more than 20,000 runs across all formats (Test matches, ODIs, and T20 matches) at an average of over 50.

Fastest to 10,000 ODI Runs:

He achieved this milestone in just 10 years and 68 days, surpassing Rahul Dravid’s record.


T20 Records:

Kohli captained India in 17 T20I bilateral series, winning 12 of them. He became the first Indian cricketer to cross 10,000 T20 runs.

50 Wins in Each Format:

Kohli is the first-ever player to be part of 50 wins in each of the three formats.


Biggest Test Win in Terms of Runs:

Under Kohli’s captaincy, India defeated New Zealand by 372 runs in the 2nd Test match of the IND vs. NZ 2021 series, marking the biggest win in terms of runs in Indian test history.

Back-to-Back Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy Wins:

Kohli, along with Ricky Ponting, is the only cricketer to win the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy in successive years.


First Indian Captain to Win Two Boxing Day Test Matches:

Kohli achieved this feat in matches against Australia and South Africa.

Test Captaincy Record:

As a Test captain, Kohli scored 5,864 runs in 113 innings at an average of 54.80, including 20 centuries and 18 fifties.


Overall Captaincy Record:

In international cricket, Kohli captained in 213 matches, winning 135 of them and scoring 12,883 runs at an average of 59.92, with 41 centuries.

ICC World Cup Performances:

In the 2011, 2015, and 2019 ICC Cricket World Cups, Kohli played crucial roles for India, amassing runs and leading the team with distinction.


Fastest to 8,000 ODI Runs:

Kohli achieved this milestone in just 175 innings, surpassing the previous record held by Sourav Ganguly.

72nd Century in ODIs:

On December 10, 2022, Kohli surpassed Ricky Ponting’s 71-century mark in ODIs.


ICC Men’s Player of the Month Award:

Kohli received this award for his outstanding performance in the Asia Cup 2022 and the ICC T20 World Cup 2022.

Second-Highest Runs Against Australia:

In the 4th Test match of BGT 2023, Kohli surpassed Brian Lara to become the second player with the most runs against Australia in international cricket, behind Sachin Tendulkar.


Most Centuries in Successful ODI Chases:

Kohli has scored 33 centuries while chasing in ODIs, demonstrating his ability to perform under pressure and guide India to victories.
